# Band-Intelligence Plan — progression suggestion · arrangement recipes · guitar realism

*2026-06-12. Main-thread synthesis of the 3-lane design pass (chair: audio-engine-architect · harmony-theory-architect · sound-design-architect; full rulings in each agent's `.claude/agent-memory/<agent>/`). Christian's brief: (1) suggest chord progressions from style/genre/feel — more than the curated few; (2) intelligent instrument selection + mixing with player override; (3) **guitar is the priority and must be better than "passable."** Follows the Jam redesign (`docs/jam-mode-roundtable.md`, slice J-1 shipped) and absorbs the backing-engine plan's unbuilt step 6 (palettes→recipes).*

**The honest ceiling (chair, stated once):** sampled instruments + a credible amp sim cap at *a good backing/demo band*, not a mic'd studio record — past that is a generative-audio app we are not building. Everything below is the best achievable inside that ceiling, and the ceiling is high enough for the job.

## Track A — Progression-suggestion engine (harmony)

**Architecture: a style-conditioned FUNCTIONAL-MOVE GRAMMAR** — not Markov (no licensed corpus; weights are unauditable and unvettable; no teaching trace), not templates-with-slots (that's what we ship today; it re-skins, never generates motion).

- `PROGRESSION_GRAMMARS` (sibling of `COMMON_PROGRESSIONS`): per style `{ cadence, start, moves (weighted T/S/D transitions), realize (function → the style's degree TOKENS — where the color lives: rock's ♭VII, jazz's tritone-sub, blues' all-dominant), idioms[] }` + a shared idiom registry (`secondary_dom`, `passing_dim`, `tritone_sub`, `truck_driver`, `modal_vamp`).
- `suggestProgression({style, feel, length, tier, seed})` → `{ tokens, label, trace }`. Tokens are first-class degree-token arrays `compileChordTimeline` already eats (cached under a generated key, so the Jam picker/overview treat a suggestion identically to an authored progression). `label` = auto roman numerals; **`trace` = the one-line why** ("vi borrowed from the parallel minor → the bittersweet lift") — the move that fired IS the explanation, so the teaching layer costs nothing.
- **`feel` maps to mode + borrowing, not new logic:** bright = major/Mixo/Lydian + plagal/♭VII · dark = Aeolian/Phrygian + ♭VI/♭II · bittersweet = major with one forced borrowed-iv/♭VI · tense = secondary dominants + passing dims + a deceptive cadence. `tier` rides the existing knobs (triad → 7ths → 9ths+tritoneSub, capped where voiceChord already caps).
- **Hybrid, not free-running:** the curated progressions remain the proven FORMS (12-bar, ii–V–I, rhythm changes, Andalusian); the grammar varies within their slots and generates fresh vamps only where a style has no rigid form. Junk is excluded structurally: authored weights (a styleless move is simply absent), function-typed motion, and a cadence fence.
- **Guards + vetting:** `assertProgressionGrammarsValid` at load (every realize token roots+voices; the moves graph reaches its cadence; per-style forbidden list — modal styles never get a functional dom-V; seeded determinism). Per-genre vetting batch of `moves`/`realize`/`idioms` by the genre-idiom agents (the harmonic sibling of the riff-vocab playbook matrix) — named, scheduled at authoring time.

## Track B — Arrangement recipes + mix intelligence (chair + sound-design)

- **`ARRANGEMENT_RECIPES`** keyed `style:feel:densityTier` (sparse|groove|full), pure POINTERS onto existing tables: `ensemble{drums,bass,comp,pad?,lead?}` (role on/off/auto + voice/engine/kit) · `registers{}` (handed to voicing) · `picks{}` (COMP_GROOVES/BASS_FIGURES/DRUM_GROOVES/AUDIO_PROFILES ids) · `mixTargets{}`. The density tiers double as the alive-band section masks (the Jam arc/section FSM consumes them for free).
- **Mix model (sound-design): three-stage gain — house × recipe × player.** The shipped 7b house mix (carves/pans/sends in `trackBus`) stays the base; a recipe declares small DELTAS (±3 dB tilts + precomputed masking rules — the arrangement knows at build time who plays, so no live analysis: bass walks → comp dips; rhythm guitar enters → keys −3 and HP up). **Touched-bus latch:** any fader/pan the player moved is never stomped by a style switch ("Band sets the mix ↔ I set the mix" + one-tap reset; faint band-target ghost ticks for honesty). Section tiers apply ramped static offsets (breakdown = comp −4, drums to spine; lift = +energy); every re-apply ramps — no sudden loudness, the limiter is never loosened.
- **Per-member swap** ("swap the drummer" — adopted from the community session-mode thread): the mixer's existing per-channel override/kit-picker precedence generalizes to comp/bass/pad voice + per-member on/off; recipe sets the default, the player's override wins.

## Track C — GUITAR, better than passable (the priority)

**Sourcing bake-off (2026-06-12 addendum — live-researched; full matrix in audio-engine's memory `project_guitar_sourcing_bakeoff`):** Christian widened the mandate to "any sample type / open-source instrument engine, as good as it gets without creating our own samples or requiring paid VSTi." Verdicts: the popular community djent library is **license-blocked** (its terms bar redistributing the sound data — out without written permission); **the win is a CC0 guitar library with a real electric DI layer incl. muted plucks + round-robins** — CC0 permits shipping a slimmed derived subset (~2RR/1-2 vel, OGG, a few MB, self-hosted in git — NOT gitignored, the license is clean); player tech = **vendor one MIT JS sampler file** (plays SF2 + SFZ-like region presets with round-robin) rather than compiling a full SFZ engine to WASM (reserve that only if full-opcode fidelity is ever needed; true VST3 hosting is out in a web plugin); Karplus-Strong hybrid mutes **rejected** (an engine to maintain that won't beat a recorded CC0 muted pluck). **Staging: the GM step still ships first** — it proves the artic-switch + amp-chain plumbing end-to-end, which the sample path reuses (open-vs-muted becomes two sample regions instead of two GM programs).

1. **C1 — the articulation voice switch (S–M).** Open-ring hits on the clean-electric preset; palm-mutes/chucks/chugs on the **muted-electric GM program** (~30KB, one file — *the lone open sourcing item*), selected per hit from tags the comp cells already carry, with a fast-attack/short-hold envelope on the sampler's existing gain node (zero extra audio nodes). Country/folk ride the already-hosted nylon/steel. Strum realization = `emitStrum` stagger width/direction + per-string velocity tilt driven by the cell's accents. **→ Christian by-ear sign-off** (the audio-realism Phase A precedent).
2. **C2 — double-track (M).** Two voices, hard L/R (±0.35 pan per sound-design's seat), ~12–20ms seed-offset + ~±6¢ detune — the single biggest "produced" lift. Live for clean; render-only for distorted (≤1 amp instance live, always). Keys auto-drop −3 when the guitar enters; level seat between bass and keys. **→ by-ear gate.**
2b. **C2-sample — the realism ceiling (M).** Vendor the MIT sampler file; curate the **CC0 derived guitar subset** (electric DI: open + muted-pluck regions, slimmed + OGG-encoded, a few MB under `static/`) as the higher-realism comp/lead voice, routed through the same artic-switch logic and the same WaveShaper/IR/NAM chain. Curation + the slim is audio-engine's task; mix seat → sound-design; per-style artic calls stay in the COMP_GROOVES cell data. **→ by-ear gate vs the GM voice (A/B).**
3. **C3 — the distorted lane (M, asset-gated).** In-house WaveShaper (tanh/asym) → tone-stack biquads → Convolver (CC0 cab IR) → post-EQ as first-class; NAM via nam_tone as the feature-detect free upgrade; the distorted bus gets its own soft comp (−16/3:1/8ms) pre-group. **The one true blocker: a cleared CC0 4×12 close-mic IR (~2–4KB)** — the highest-leverage single asset for guitar realism. Until cleared, distorted keeps the synth-pad placeholder. **→ by-ear gate.**

## Build order (guitar-first, honoring the one dependency)

The recipe ensemble slot (B1) is the substrate every guitar voice attaches to, and it's pure data — so:

- **Phase 1:** B1 recipe registry + resolver (data only, mirrors `resolveAudioProfile`) **+ C1 the guitar articulation voice** → the first by-ear moment as early as possible. *Start the asset hunt NOW in parallel: the muted-electric GM file + the CC0 cab IR (C3's gate).*
- **Phase 2:** C2 double-track (by-ear) ‖ A1 the grammar + `suggestProgression` (independent of guitar; lands the "Suggest changes" button + feel control in Jam). C2-sample (the CC0 subset + vendored sampler) starts here too — its by-ear A/B against the GM voice decides which becomes the default guitar.
- **Phase 3:** B2 mix targets + touched-latch + per-member swaps (the "intelligent mixing with the option to switch" ask, complete).
- **Phase 4:** C3 distorted chain the moment the IR clears (unlocks rock/metal/djent jams sounding real) + the genre vetting batch for the grammar tables.

**Consults owed at build time:** genre-idiom agents (grammar vetting batch + recipe authenticity), rhythm-meter (harmonic-rhythm/key-shift seam timing), ux (feel control + trace display + swap surface), guitar-pedagogy (nothing here is scored or fingered — display/audio only, no pass needed unless a guide surface changes).
